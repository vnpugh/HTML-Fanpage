The Revolution Tribute Page!

Bio

The Revolution was formed in 1979 by Prince in Minneapolis, MN. Though Prince first teased the band’s name on the 1999 album cover, the Revolution was given the full spotlight on Purple Rain, which went on to sell more than 20 million copies, spend 24 weeks at Number One on the album charts, and win two Grammys and an Oscar. The project marked the first time ever that an artist had a Number One album, single, and film simultaneously.

Following a tour that put them in front of sold-out stadium crowds, the Revolution backed Prince on his next two groundbreaking albums, Around the World In a Day and Parade. The group’s final recording on a Prince album is the show-stopping live track “It’s Gonna Be a Beautiful Night” on 1987’s Sign O the Times. After Prince’s death in April 2016, the Revolution immediately re-connected, but stayed out of the public eye until a series of three triumphant, emotional shows at the historic First Avenue club in September. Fans flew in from around the world to witness the band at the site where the concert scenes in Purple Rain were filmed.

Band Members

  • Prince on lead vocals, guitar and piano (1979-1986)
  • Dez Dickerson on guitar (1979-1983)
  • Matt Fink on keyboards and vocals (1979–1986, 2012, 2016–present)
  • Lisa Coleman on keyboards and vocals (1979–1986, 2012, 2016–present)
  • Bobby Z. on drums and percussion (1979–1986, 2012, 2016–present)
  • André Cymone on bass guitar and backing vocals (1979–1981)
  • Wendy Melvoin on rhythm guitar and vocals (1983–1986, 2012, 2016–present)
